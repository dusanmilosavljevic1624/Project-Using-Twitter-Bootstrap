Bloodbath

Ankara, Turkey—Survivors stand among the dead and injured after twin explosions tore through a rally of pro-Kurdish activists calling for an end to fighting between the Turkish military and Kurdish armed groups, October 10. At least 97 people were killed, Turkish authorities said. Other estimates put the number as high as 128, with hundreds more wounded. Turkish Prime Minister Ahmet Davutoglu blamed ISIS militants for the suicide bombs. The attack comes at a critical time for Turkey, which is facing snap elections on November 1 and an increase in fighting between security forces and Kurdish militants, who are also battling ISIS in Syria and Iraq.
